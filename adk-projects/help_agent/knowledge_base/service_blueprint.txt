CareConnect – Service Blueprint: As-Is and To-Be Processes
===========================================================

AS-IS PROCESS (BEFORE CARECONNECT)
-----------------------------------
The following describes the monthly transport reimbursement process as experienced by legal guardians and self-managing IV claimants prior to CareConnect. This was mapped through stakeholder interviews and service blueprint analysis.

Step 1 — Annual Setup
The guardian manually creates a transport schedule in Excel listing all planned rides for the year. This spreadsheet is emailed to TixiTaxi. If appointments change mid-year, the guardian emails updates manually. There is no confirmation system — TixiTaxi may or may not acknowledge receipt.

Step 2 — Monthly Ride Tracking
Each month, the guardian tracks which rides actually happened and which were cancelled. This is done from memory, a separate notebook, or by reviewing emails. There is no centralised log. Cancelled rides that were not communicated in time may still appear on the TixiTaxi invoice.

Step 3 — Waiting for TixiTaxi Invoice
The TixiTaxi invoice arrives by email or post, typically mid-month after the previous month's rides. The guardian cannot begin Form 5050 until this arrives.

Step 4 — Waiting for Meal Invoice
The day centre meal invoice arrives separately, sometimes on a different schedule. The guardian must hold both invoices in working memory and wait until both are present before proceeding.

Step 5 — Manual Form 5050 Completion
Once both invoices are available, the guardian manually fills in Form 5050. Each trip requires a separate row with: travel date, appointment reason, appointment address, transport type, cost or distance, and total. The rules for which column to fill — public transport, private car, or taxi/other — must be applied correctly for each row. Errors here cause rejections.

Step 6 — Sign and Submit
The completed form is printed or exported, signed, and submitted through the SVA portal. Original receipts are attached.

Step 7 — Rejection Without Explanation
If something is wrong — a missing field, incorrect transport column, unapproved provider, wrong amount — the guardian receives a rejection weeks later. The rejection rarely specifies which row or field failed. The guardian must re-examine the entire submission to find the error.

Key friction points identified:
- The stateful waiting problem: no system tracks whether all required invoices have arrived
- The matching problem: linking meal invoices to the correct transport dates is done manually
- The classification problem: knowing which SVA column applies for each transport type requires rule knowledge the system does not provide
- The feedback gap: rejections are delayed and non-specific

TO-BE PROCESS (WITH CARECONNECT)
---------------------------------

Step 1 — Annual Ride Schedule Setup
The guardian logs all planned rides in the CareConnect Ride Planning page, including date, time, pickup, destination, and appointment type. When ready, one click sends the full schedule to TixiTaxi via a structured email generated by the Email Agent. No manual spreadsheet. No manual email composition.

Step 2 — Monthly Ride Tracking
CareConnect maintains a live log of upcoming and past rides. Completed rides are visible in the records. If a ride needs to be cancelled, the guardian clicks Cancel Ride in the interface and the Twilio Agent places an automated outbound call to TixiTaxi's cancellation line in Swiss German on the guardian's behalf.

Step 3 — Receipt Upload and Extraction
When a TixiTaxi invoice or transport receipt arrives, the guardian uploads it to CareConnect. The Invoice Extraction Agent reads the document using vision AI, extracts the travel date, CHF amount, and transport type, and presents these values for review. The agent then asks the guardian for the appointment reason and care provider address — the two fields that cannot be extracted from a receipt.

Step 4 — Automatic Form Row Assembly
The agent assembles a validated Form 5050 row from the extracted and confirmed data, applying the correct SVA column (public transport, private car, or taxi/other), calculating totals, and flagging any anomalies. The guardian reviews the row before it is saved to the records.

Step 5 — Invoice Matching and Status Visibility
The Invoice Records page shows which transport invoices have been matched to a meal receipt and which have not. Unmatched invoices are flagged visibly. The guardian can see at a glance whether their documentation is complete for the current month.

Step 6 — Form 5050 Generation
Once all receipts are logged and matched, the guardian uses the Generate SVA Form 5050 function to produce a complete, print-ready Form 5050 pre-filled from their records. They review it, add their signature, and submit through the SVA portal.

HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP DESIGN
--------------------------
A deliberate principle running through the to-be process is that the agent prepares and the human approves. This was not just a design preference — it emerged directly from user research. When participants were told an agent could send things on their behalf automatically, trust broke down immediately. They needed to know what was sent and to have triggered it themselves.

The following actions always require explicit user confirmation before execution:
- Sending the ride schedule email to TixiTaxi
- Cancelling a ride (triggering the Twilio call)
- Saving an extracted invoice row to records
- Generating and printing the final Form 5050

The agent does not act autonomously on any consequential step. Its role is to reduce the data entry, classification, and matching work — not to remove the human from the process.

WHERE THE AGENT TAKES OVER (SUMMARY)
--------------------------------------
- Reading and extracting receipt data (date, amount, transport type)
- Classifying which SVA transport column applies
- Assembling complete Form 5050 rows with correct totals
- Composing and sending the annual ride schedule to TixiTaxi
- Placing automated cancellation calls to TixiTaxi
- Matching meal receipts to transport appointment dates
- Flagging missing or unmatched invoices
- Answering eligibility and classification questions via the Help Agent

WHERE THE HUMAN REMAINS IN THE LOOP (SUMMARY)
-----------------------------------------------
- Confirming the appointment reason and care provider for each trip
- Reviewing and approving each extracted invoice row before saving
- Triggering all outbound actions (email send, cancellation call, form generation)
- Final review of the completed Form 5050 before SVA portal submission
- Actual submission through the SVA portal
