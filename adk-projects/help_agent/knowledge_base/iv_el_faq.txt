# Swiss IV/EL Disability Benefits — Frequently Asked Questions

## About IV (Invalidenversicherung)

**What is IV?**
IV (Invalidenversicherung) is Switzerland's federal disability insurance. It provides
benefits to people whose ability to work or carry out daily activities is permanently
impaired due to illness, accident, or congenital condition. IV is administered
federally but delivered through the 26 kantonal SVA Durchführungsstellen.

**What transport costs does IV cover?**
IV covers necessary transport to approved medical and therapeutic treatments:
- Travel to doctors, hospitals, physiotherapists, occupational therapists, and other
  approved treatment providers named in the IV decision (Verfügung).
- Reimbursed via SVA Form 5050 using one of three transport types:
  OV (public transport), Privatauto (private car at CHF 0.70/km), or Taxi.

**Who qualifies for IV transport reimbursement?**
Persons who hold a valid IV Verfügung that includes Behandlungskostenvergütung
(treatment cost reimbursement). If you are unsure whether your child's Verfügung
includes transport, contact your kantonal SVA office.

**What is a Verfügung?**
A Verfügung is the official IV/EL decision document. It specifies:
- Which benefits you are entitled to
- Which treatment providers are approved
- The duration and scope of the entitlement
Always check your Verfügung before assuming a trip is covered.

## About EL (Ergänzungsleistungen)

**What is EL?**
EL (Ergänzungsleistungen) are supplementary benefits paid on top of IV/AHV to cover
basic living costs that IV alone does not fully cover. EL is means-tested — entitlement
depends on income and assets.

**What does EL cover that IV does not?**
EL can cover:
- Meal costs at approved day structures, care centres, or institutions
- Home care costs beyond what IV reimburses
- Specific living expenses defined in the EL decision

**How are meal costs submitted?**
Meal costs are tracked in CareConnect under the **Meal** category. They are submitted
on the EL claim form (not Form 5050). Check with your kantonal SVA office for the
correct EL reimbursement form and submission deadline for your canton.

## Documentation & Records

**What receipts must I keep?**
Keep original receipts for all transport and meal costs for at least **5 years**.
The SVA can request them during an audit at any time.

**What information must a receipt show?**
A valid receipt should show:
- Date of travel or service
- Amount paid in CHF
- Provider name (taxi company, SBB, etc.)

If a receipt is missing or illegible, note the trip in writing with date, route,
amount, and reason — and keep this with your records.

**Can I submit scanned or photographed receipts?**
For CareConnect processing, yes — upload a clear photo or PDF. For the SVA submission,
check with your kantonal office whether they accept digital copies or require originals.

## Submission & Deadlines

**When should I submit Form 5050?**
Submit by the **end of the month following** the month of treatment. For example,
trips in March should be submitted by the end of April.

**What happens if I submit late?**
Late submissions may be rejected by the SVA. If you have a good reason for the delay,
contact your SVA office before the deadline to request an extension.

**Can I submit multiple months at once?**
Generally no — submit monthly as required. If you have missed a month, contact your
SVA office; they may accept a catch-up submission with documentation.

**Where do I submit?**
To the kantonal SVA Durchführungsstelle that manages your IV/EL case. The correct
office is named in your Verfügung. Contact details: [www.ahv-iv.ch](https://www.ahv-iv.ch)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correct approach |
|---|---|
| Filling two cost columns (e.g. both OV and Taxi) for the same row | Use exactly one cost column per row |
| Claiming full train fare when you have a Halbtaxabo | Use the reduced price you actually paid |
| Submitting a trip to a provider not in the Verfügung | Confirm approval with SVA before the appointment |
| Missing the Behandlungsgrund or Behandlungsort | Always include both — the SVA will reject incomplete rows |
| Losing original receipts | Photograph receipts immediately and store in CareConnect |
| Submitting meal costs on Form 5050 | Meals are covered under EL, not Form 5050 |

## Getting Help

  or SVA rules.

  or submission deadlines. Find your office at [www.ahv-iv.ch](https://www.ahv-iv.ch).
